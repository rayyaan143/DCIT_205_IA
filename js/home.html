EMBARK ON A JOURNEY OF EXCELLENCE IN COMPUTER SCIENCE

WHY STUDY COMPUTER SCIENCE?

Scholars in computer science ask questions like, "Can we build computers that think like humans?" How might computer games with growing characters be improved? What does the future
of augmented reality look like?Computer science is a theoretical and practical problem-solving discipline that combines creativity, mathematics, logic, and communication. A solid understanding
of computer science principles can help you prepare for a lengthy career of problem solving and system design without the need for ongoing training.Throughout your study, you will be actively
solving issues and developing new programs in class and tutorial laboratories. Students interested in acquiring expertise in computer science research might look for volunteer or paid opportunities to work in research laboratories.


Certificate In Programming

The Certificate in Programming teaches undergraduates how to program computers
to help them with their studies in any field of study. While the Certificate does
not provide the same theoretical and system foundation as computer science degrees,
it does provide a quick path to programming literacy that may be completed in four terms.



Graduate Program

The Department offers two graduate programs: a doctoral program leading to
the degree of Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) and a terminal master's program
leading to the degree of Master of Science (M.S.). The doctoral program is
designed for students who want to work in education or research.
